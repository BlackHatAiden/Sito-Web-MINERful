Constraints subsumption (strictness) hierarchy


This image represents the hierarchy of the various constraints and we analyze the two figures:

  • Figure a represents Existence constraints where:

    • init(x) and end(x) are considered Position templates because they define the initial and final position of the variable x.

    • Participation(x) and atMostOne(x) are considered Cardinality templates because they define the cardinality with which the variable x is referenced.


  • Figure b represents Relation constraints where:

    • RespondedExistance(y,x), Precedence(x,y), AlternatePrecedence(x,y) and ChainPrecedence(x,y) are considered Backward-undirectional relation templates because they define that if the variable y occurs then previously must occur the variable x.

    • CoExistance(y,x), Succession(x,y), AlternateSuccession(x,y) and ChainSuccession(x,y) are considered Coupling templates because they define that if the variable x occurs then subsequently must occur the variable y.

    • RespondedExistance(x,y), Response(x,y), AlternateResponse(x,y) and ChainResponse(x,y) are considered Forward-undirectional relation templates because they define the coexistence between constraints such as RespondedExistence(x, y) and RespondedExistence(y, x).

    • NotCoExistance(x,y), NotSuccession(x,y) and NotChainSuccession(x,y) are considered Negative templates because they define the absence in the immediate succession or in the chain of successions of the variable y after the variable x.
